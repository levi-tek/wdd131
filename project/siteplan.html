Site Name:

The name of the site is (LOVAS), it is an acronym of the phrase (Levi Oguike virtual assistant services)

Site Purpose:

The website will focus on showcasing virtual assistant services, specifically targeting small business owners and entrepreneurs. It will include:

1. About the Service: An overview of what virtual assistant services are and how they help businesses.

2. Services Offered: A list of tasks, such as email management, social media scheduling, calendar organization, and customer support.

3. Client Testimonials: Reviews and feedback from satisfied clients.

4. Pricing and Packages: Clear details about pricing plans and what’s included in each package.

5. Contact and Booking: A section for potential clients to book a consultation or inquire about services.

Scenarios:

1. How can a virtual assistant help me save time and focus on growing my business?

* This will guide the "About the Service" and "Services Offered" sections, explaining how virtual assistants manage tasks like email organization, calendar management, and social media scheduling to help business owners focus on strategic growth.

2. What are your pricing options, and how do I know which package is right for my business?

* This question will shape the "Pricing and Packages" section, offering detailed information on service tiers and recommending the best options based on client needs.

3. Can you handle customer inquiries and support for my business?

* This will be addressed in the "Services Offered" section, highlighting customer support as part of the services available.

4. Do you have any testimonials or reviews from past clients?

* This will influence the "Client Testimonials" section, showcasing positive feedback to build trust and credibility.

5. What is the process for getting started with your services?

* This will guide the "Contact and Booking" section, providing a step-by-step process for inquiries, consultations, and onboarding.

6. Can you help me manage and schedule content for my social media accounts?

This question will be answered under "Services Offered," detailing how social media management is one of the services provided.

7. What industries or types of businesses do you specialize in working with?

This will ensure the website includes content about Levi's expertise in specific industries, catering to a diverse target audience.

8. How do I contact you if I have more questions about your services?

This will shape the "Contact and Booking" section to include easy-to-use contact forms and clear communication channels.

Color Schema:

1. Primary Color: Blue (#004AAD)

Usage:

Headings and Titles: Used for all primary headers (H1, H2) to emphasize professionalism.

Navigation Bar: Background color to maintain a strong and authoritative look.

Call-to-Action Buttons: Background color for buttons like "Book Now" or "Learn More" to create consistency.

Footer: Solid background for a clean and structured finish at the bottom of the page.

2. Secondary Color: Light Gray (#F2F2F2)

Usage:

Background: Used for section backgrounds to create contrast with the primary color.

Paragraphs: Subtle background for text-heavy sections, making content easy on the eyes.

Borders and Dividers: Thin lines to separate content and maintain structure without overwhelming.

Secondary Buttons: For less emphasized actions like “View More” or “Cancel.”

3. Accent Color: Red (#FF3131)

Usage:

Highlights: To emphasize key text or headings, such as pricing details or special offers.

Hyperlinks and Hover Effects: Adds energy and visibility for clickable elements.

Icons or Graphics: Small touches like checkmarks or section dividers to add vibrancy.

CTA Buttons (Hover): When hovered over, buttons change to orange to grab attention.

4. Neutral Color: White (#FFFFFF)

Usage:

Main Background: Ensures content is readable and the site feels open and uncluttered.

Text Boxes and Forms: Used in input fields for booking consultations or inquiries.

Contrast Against Blue and Red: Keeps the design clean and modern.

5. Support Color: Dark Gray (#4D4D4D)

Usage:

Body Text: To avoid the harshness of black for readability in paragraphs.

Secondary Icons and Buttons: For understated features like back-to-top buttons or disclaimers.

Footer Text: Subdued text in the footer for copyright or additional links.

Implementation Notes:

The primary and accent colors will create a professional yet engaging atmosphere, while the secondary and neutral colors provide balance and clarity.

Hover animations with accent colors will improve user interactivity and engagement.

The color balance will ensure that important elements (like CTAs and headings) stand out while maintaining a cohesive design.

Typography:

1. Primary Font (Body Text)

Roboto

Type: Sans-serif

Why: Roboto is clean, modern, and highly readable across devices. It has a neutral tone, making it perfect for body text where legibility is key.

Use: This font will be used for the body text, including main content, paragraphs, services descriptions, other text-heavy sections, and general content across the website. Roboto is easy to read and has a modern, neutral style that works well for extended reading.

2. Secondary Font (Headings and subheadings)

Merriweather

Type: Serif

Why: Merriweather is a serif font that adds a touch of elegance while still being very readable. It contrasts nicely with the clean, modern feel of Roboto, giving your headings and subheadings a distinguished appearance.

Use: This font will be used for headings (H1, H2, H3) and subheadings to create visual hierarchy and guide the user through the content.

3. Accent Font (Special Sections)

Montserrat

Type: Sans-serif

Why: Montserrat is bold and modern, making it perfect for call-to-action buttons, highlighted text, or navigation elements. It adds emphasis without overwhelming the content.

Use: Montserrat will be used for special sections, such as call-to-action buttons, quotes, testimonials, or navigation menus.

Font Hierarchy and Application:

Headings: Use Merriweather for all main headings (H1) and subheadings (H2, H3). Different weights (e.g., Bold for H1, Regular for H2) will create emphasis and structure on the page.

Body Text: Roboto will be used for paragraphs and general body content, ensuring readability and clarity for the users.

Special Sections: Montserrat will be used for buttons, navigation, and highlighted text to create emphasis and focus on interactive elements.
